Agencies & Parastatals

Nigeria's Agencies and Parastatals extend the Ministry's reach by specialising in training, research, conflict resolution, diaspora engagement and technical cooperation.

Under the Ministry

Our Agencies & Parastatals

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs superintends five specialised agencies and parastatals, each with a distinct mandate that complements and extends the Ministry's core diplomatic and foreign policy functions. Click any agency below to learn more about its mandate and impact.

Foreign Service Academy (FSA)

Diplomatic Training & Professional Development

The Foreign Service Academy is the premier training institution for Nigeria's diplomatic corps and foreign service officers. It designs and delivers training programmes — including induction courses, specialist workshops and executive leadership programmes — that equip Nigerian diplomats with the skills, knowledge and values they need to represent the nation effectively at home and abroad. The FSA also hosts regional and international training programmes in partnership with foreign diplomatic academies and international organisations.

Nigeria Institute of International Affairs (NIIA)

Africa's Premier Foreign Policy Think Tank

Established in 1961, the Nigerian Institute of International Affairs (NIIA) is Africa's foremost foreign policy think tank and research institution. It conducts research, produces policy papers and convenes high-level conferences on international relations, foreign policy, peace and security, and global governance. The NIIA's publications and research outputs inform Nigerian foreign policy formulation, contribute to academic scholarship and raise the quality of public debate on international affairs in Nigeria and across the continent.

Nigerian Institute for Peace and Conflict Resolution (NIPCR)

Peacebuilding & Conflict Management

The Nigerian Institute for Peace and Conflict Resolution (NIPCR) is the Federal Government's dedicated centre for peacebuilding, conflict prevention and management. It provides early warning analysis on conflict situations across Nigeria and the African continent, designs and delivers conflict resolution and peacebuilding programmes, and builds the capacity of government agencies, civil society organisations and communities to manage disputes through dialogue and non-violent means. NIPCR plays a key role in Nigeria's broader commitment to peace on the African continent.

Nigerians in Diaspora Commission (NiDCOM)

Diaspora Engagement & Welfare Protection

The Nigerians in Diaspora Commission (NiDCOM) is the Federal Government agency responsible for engaging, connecting and protecting the interests of the estimated 17 million Nigerians living abroad. It coordinates diaspora investment mobilisation, facilitates the transfer of skills and remittances from Nigerians overseas, advocates for the welfare and rights of Nigerians abroad, and serves as the primary bridge between the Federal Government and the global Nigerian diaspora community. NiDCOM is central to implementing the "Diaspora" pillar of the Ministry's 4D Doctrine.

Technical Aid Corps (TAC)

South–South Technical Cooperation

The Technical Aid Corps (TAC) is Nigeria's flagship South–South technical cooperation programme. Established in 1987, it deploys Nigerian professionals — including doctors, engineers, educators, lawyers and other technical experts — to developing countries in Africa, the Caribbean and the Pacific that request Nigerian expertise. The TAC is a powerful expression of Nigeria's commitment to African solidarity and South–South cooperation, demonstrating that Nigeria's foreign policy is not merely transactional but grounded in a genuine commitment to the upliftment of developing nations worldwide.

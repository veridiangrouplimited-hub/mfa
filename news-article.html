Keynote Address by H.E. Bianca Odumegwu-Ojukwu, Honourable Minister of Foreign Affairs

June 15, 2026 Office of the Honourable Minister 4 min read
Back to Press Center

The Honourable Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ambassador Bianca Odumegwu-Ojukwu, delivered a wide-ranging keynote address on Nigeria's diplomatic priorities, the 4D foreign policy agenda and the country's vision for a more engaged and equitable global order.

Speaking at a gathering of diplomats, policy experts and civil society representatives, the Minister reaffirmed Nigeria's commitment to citizen-centred diplomacy — a hallmark of the current administration's approach to foreign engagement. She outlined four pillars of the 4D Agenda: Diaspora, Democracy, Development and Diplomacy — as guiding principles for Nigeria's international relations going forward.

"Nigeria is not merely a participant in global affairs — we are an architect of the international order that serves all of humanity," the Minister declared. "Our diplomacy is rooted in the values of our people: dignity, enterprise and an unshakeable belief in the continent of Africa's rightful place at the global table."

"We are building a foreign service that serves 220 million Nigerians — at home, in the diaspora, and on the world stage. That is the standard to which we hold ourselves." — H.E. Bianca Odumegwu-Ojukwu, Honourable Minister of Foreign Affairs

Diaspora Engagement

The Minister highlighted the critical role of the Nigerian diaspora — estimated at over 17 million globally — as a strategic asset in economic diplomacy. She announced new measures to streamline consular services, improve document authentication turnaround times and establish diaspora advisory councils across key hubs including London, Houston, Johannesburg and Dubai.

Multilateral Leadership

On the multilateral front, Ambassador Odumegwu-Ojukwu underscored Nigeria's renewed engagement with the African Union, ECOWAS and the United Nations. She pointed to Nigeria's election to the UN Human Rights Council as a demonstration of the country's credibility on the world stage and pledged that Nigeria would use its seat to advocate for the rights of developing nations, gender equality and climate justice.

Economic Diplomacy

The keynote also addressed Nigeria's economic diplomacy strategy, with the Minister unveiling plans for investment summits in partnership with the Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission. She noted that 12 new trade agreements are under negotiation with partners across Asia, Latin America and the Gulf Cooperation Council — targeting a 40% increase in non-oil export revenues by 2027.

The address concluded with a call to all Nigerians — at home and in the diaspora — to see themselves as ambassadors of the nation, upholding its values and contributing to its development wherever they are in the world.

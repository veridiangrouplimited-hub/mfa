History of Nigeria

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, gained independence from British colonial rule on 1 October 1960 and became a republic in 1963. Today it is a federation of 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory, Abuja, and the most populous nation in Africa.

From its pre-colonial kingdoms and empires through independence, civil war, military rule and the return to democracy in 1999, Nigeria has grown into a continental leader in diplomacy, economy and culture.

Natural Resources

Nigeria is richly endowed with natural resources. It is one of the world's leading producers of crude oil and natural gas, and holds significant deposits of solid minerals including tin, columbite, iron ore, coal, limestone, lead, zinc and gold, alongside vast arable land and water resources.

Investment

As Africa's largest economy by several measures, Nigeria offers a dynamic market of over 200 million people, a growing technology sector and expanding opportunities across agriculture, energy, manufacturing, infrastructure and the creative industries. The Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission supports investors entering the market.

The People

Nigeria is home to more than 250 ethnic groups and over 500 languages, with English as the official language. This remarkable diversity — including the Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo and many other peoples — is a source of national strength, creativity and resilience.

Culture

Nigerian culture is globally influential, from Nollywood — one of the world's largest film industries — to Afrobeats music, literature, fashion and cuisine. A vibrant tradition of festivals, art and storytelling continues to shape the nation's identity at home and abroad.

Tourism

Nigeria offers diverse attractions: the beaches of Lagos, the highlands of Obudu and Jos, Yankari National Park, the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), historic cities and a wealth of cultural festivals that draw visitors from across the world.

National Symbols

The Flag

Two green bands and a white centre band — green for agriculture and natural wealth, white for peace and unity.

Coat of Arms

A black shield with two supporting horses and an eagle, symbolising the nation's strength and dignity.

National Anthem

"Nigeria, We Hail Thee" — the nation's anthem, expressing unity, service and patriotism.

National Pledge

A solemn commitment to be faithful, loyal and honest, and to serve Nigeria with all one's strength.
